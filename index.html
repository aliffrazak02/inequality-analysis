Poverty, Income Inequality, and Health Outcomes Across Malaysian States

State Deprivation Index (SDI) Map

The choropleth map colours each Malaysian state by its SDI score. Darker shades indicate higher deprivation, meaning greater poverty, lower income, and worse health outcomes relative to other states. Hover over a state to see its exact score.

Income Inequality (Gini Coefficient)

The Gini coefficient measures how unevenly income is distributed within a state, ranging from 0 (perfect equality) to 1 (maximum inequality). States with a higher Gini tend to have a wider gap between their wealthiest and poorest households.

Health Outcome Indicators

Charts track key health metrics such as infant mortality rate and life expectancy alongside poverty levels. A strong correlation between high deprivation scores and poorer health outcomes highlights where policy intervention is most needed.

How to Use This Dashboard

Use the filters in the toolbar to switch between states or years. Click on any bar or map region to cross-filter all other charts. Use the tabs at the top of the dashboard to explore different views of the data.

Data sources: Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM), Ministry of Health Malaysia. SDI is a composite index derived from poverty rate, mean household income, and selected health indicators at the state level.
