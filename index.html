Poverty, Income Inequality, and Health Outcomes Across Malaysian States

About This Dashboard

This dashboard explores the relationship between poverty, income inequality, and health outcomes across all 16 Malaysian states and federal territories. The data draws on official statistics from the Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM) and the Ministry of Health Malaysia, covering household income surveys and public health records.

The central measure used throughout is the State Deprivation Index (SDI), a composite score that combines poverty rate, mean household income, and selected health indicators into a single value for each state. A higher SDI signals greater overall deprivation. This index makes it easier to compare states holistically rather than relying on any single metric in isolation.

State Deprivation Index (SDI) Map

The choropleth map on the overview tab assigns a colour to each state based on its SDI score. Darker shades indicate higher deprivation. States such as Sabah and Kelantan have historically recorded the highest SDI values, reflecting persistently elevated poverty rates and lower household incomes compared to more urbanised states like Kuala Lumpur and Selangor.

Hovering over any state reveals its exact SDI score alongside its component values. This allows you to see, for example, whether a state's high SDI is driven primarily by poverty incidence or by weak health outcomes, which can point toward different kinds of policy responses.

Income Inequality and the Gini Coefficient

The Gini coefficient is a widely used measure of income inequality. It ranges from 0, which represents a situation where every household earns exactly the same income, to 1, which represents a situation where a single household earns everything. In practice, Malaysian states fall roughly between 0.30 and 0.45.

An important distinction to keep in mind: a state can have a relatively high mean household income and still have a high Gini coefficient. This is common in states undergoing rapid urbanisation, where economic growth benefits some segments of the population much more than others. The bar charts in this section let you compare Gini values across states and track how inequality has shifted over different survey years.

Health Outcome Indicators

Health outcomes are represented through two primary indicators: infant mortality rate (deaths per 1,000 live births) and life expectancy at birth. These metrics are sensitive to the quality and accessibility of healthcare infrastructure, which itself tends to correlate with the level of economic development in a state.

The scatter plots in this section show each state plotted against both its SDI score and its health outcome values. States that cluster in the upper-right region of the chart, those with both high deprivation and poor health outcomes, are the focus areas most in need of targeted intervention. The trend line across all states illustrates the overall direction of the relationship, though individual states can deviate significantly based on local factors such as rural healthcare access or recent infrastructure investment.

How to Use This Dashboard

The dashboard is interactive. Here are the main ways to navigate and explore it:

  • Filters: Use the filter panel on the right to narrow down by state, year, or indicator. Changes apply across all charts on the active tab simultaneously.
  • Cross-filtering: Clicking on a bar, point, or map region will highlight the corresponding data in all other charts on the page. Click the same element again to clear the selection.
  • Tabs: The dashboard is organised into multiple views. The SDI Overview tab provides the high-level geographic picture, while subsequent tabs break down income and health indicators in more detail.
  • Tooltips: Hovering over any chart element shows a tooltip with the exact data values for that state and year.
  • Download: The toolbar at the bottom of the dashboard includes an option to download the current view as an image or PDF.
